A Vital Support System for Students

Pre-Matric and Post-Matric scholarships empower millions of students to pursue their educational dreams. The Government ensures this financial aid reaches students directly and on time through the Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) system. However, a small technical step often becomes a major hurdle.

Common Challenges Hindering Scholarship Utilization

Aadhaar Linking Issues

Many students face problems linking Aadhaar to bank accounts, causing failed DBT transactions. In Tripura, 1,983 ST students were rejected due to Aadhaar not being DBT-enabled.

Technical Glitches and Delays

Portal issues and delayed fund disbursal led to a decline of ~89 lakh beneficiaries. Experts attribute this to mandatory Aadhaar use and system delays.

Fund Misappropriation

Cases of fraudulent claims and fund misappropriation have been reported. In Himachal Pradesh, the CBI registered FIRs related to a multi-crore scholarship scam.

Aadhaar Linked vs. Aadhaar Seeded for DBT

Understanding this difference is crucial for receiving your scholarship without any delay. Let's break it down.

❌ Aadhaar Linked Account

This is a basic level of connection, often done for identity verification (e-KYC).

  • Purpose: Used mainly for identification and authentication.
  • Functionality: Proves you are who you say you are when opening an account or for other KYC purposes.
  • Scholarships: INSUFFICIENT for receiving government subsidies like scholarships directly.

✔️ Aadhaar Seeded & DBT Enabled

This links your Aadhaar to your bank account AND registers it with the NPCI mapper for receiving government funds.

  • Purpose: To create a direct pipeline for government benefits to your specific bank account.
  • Functionality: Enables Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT). All government payments are routed to this one designated account.
  • Scholarships: ESSENTIAL for receiving scholarship funds from schemes on the National Scholarship Portal (NSP).
